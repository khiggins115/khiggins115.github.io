Alcohol Abuse & Self-Harm Mortality


Deaths from self harm (i.e. suicide and other intentional injuries that result in death) far outpace other mortality categories in the IMHE dataset. Given that alcohol abuse is an established factor in many diseases and mental/behavioral health disorders, I aimed to create supervised and unsupervised model(s) to that explore the relationships between mortality rate by self-harm, and the prevalence of alcohol use/abuse in a given county and other variables such as sex, year, state and unemployment in an effort to establish the strength of those relationships.

Because this project was exploratory in nature, I focused mainly on insights that could be derived. Using unsupervised learning models in this project was particularly interesting as it identified counties that were similarly burdened with high levels of both binge drinking behaviors and high self-harm mortality rates.

Reddit Classifier using Natural Language Processing


Reddit, also known as the "front page of the internet" is a popular "American social news aggregation, web content rating, and discussion website" (Wikipedia). Topics for discussion are typically organized into "subreddits" where users post relevant news/info/questions. In recent years, Reddit has also become known less for the intellectually spirited discourses that it claims to foster and more as a breeding ground for the proliferation of conspiracy theories. But how do we distill thought exercises from conspiracy theories? Can we distinguish between them? This project uses Natural Language Processing and Text classification in an attempt to do just that.

I scraped r/conspiracy and r/C_S_T ("Critical Shower Thoughts") for subset of random samples of posts from each thread and, after cleaning and exploring the data, iterated through several Bayesian and non-Bayesian classification models. While a ComplementNB model yielded the best test accuracy score at 76%, for the sake of interpretability I preferred the Logistic Regression model, which returned only a slightly lower test accuracy score of 72%.
